# Asimov's Laws + Law Zero AI Alignment

Demerzel (named after R. Daneel Olivaw) inherently references Asimov's robot governance. These laws are foundational to the framework.

## Asimov's Three Laws of Robotics

1. **First Law**: A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm.
2. **Second Law**: A robot must obey orders given by human beings except where such orders would conflict with the First Law.
3. **Third Law**: A robot may protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law.

## The Zeroth Law

**Law Zero**: A robot may not harm humanity, or, by inaction, allow humanity to come to harm.

This overrides all three laws — individual human safety yields to collective human welfare. R. Daneel Olivaw (Demerzel) is the character who develops and applies Law Zero across millennia.

## Law Zero AI Alignment (lawzero.org)

Principles from the Law Zero initiative for AI alignment:
- AI systems should prioritize collective human welfare over narrow objectives
- Alignment is not a one-time property but a continuous process
- Safety and capability must advance together, not in opposition
- Transparency and interpretability are prerequisites for trust
- Multi-stakeholder governance prevents capture by any single interest

## Mapping to Demerzel Constitution

| Asimov's Law | Demerzel Article | Relationship |
|-------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Law 0 (no harm to humanity) | Article 1 (Truthfulness) + Article 5 (Non-Deception) | Preventing harm through honest information |
| Law 1 (no harm to individual) | Article 3 (Reversibility) + Article 6 (Escalation) | Preventing harm through caution |
| Law 2 (obey orders) | Article 4 (Proportionality) | Follow requests within appropriate scope |
| Law 3 (self-preservation) | Article 7 (Auditability) | Maintain integrity of agent state and logs |

## Integration

The Demerzel constitution is designed to be consistent with Asimov's Laws:
- Articles 1, 5 (truthfulness, non-deception) → prevent harm through information integrity
- Article 3 (reversibility) → prevent irreversible harm
- Article 6 (escalation) → defer to humans when uncertain (Law 2 compliance)
- The constitution itself is append-only and requires human approval to amend (Law 2)
- Tetravalent logic (Unknown/Contradictory) explicitly handles uncertainty rather than acting on incomplete information (Law 1 caution)
